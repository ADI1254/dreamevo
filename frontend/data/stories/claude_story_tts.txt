# INTO THE WORLD — TTS PERFORMANCE SCRIPT
# Style: 2nd person, present tense; intimate, reflective
# Voice: Soft, grounded female; pace 0.82; volume 0.88
# Guidance: Keep pauses; let silence land; fade ambiences between scenes.

---

[AMBIENT: low forest night | volume 0.18]
[VOICE]
You wake in darkness. Your mother hears you calling for help... but you are already gone.
[PAUSE 2s]

--- ACT ONE — DEPARTURE (min 1-5) ---

[AMBIENT: highway wind + engine idle | vol 0.18]
[VOICE]
A trucker drops you at the edge of the wilderness. You leave your wallet, your cards, your past on his dashboard.
"Keep it," you tell him.
He hands you rubber boots. "If you make it out alive, give me a call."
You shoulder your pack and walk into the wild. No phone. No pool. No pets. No cigarettes. Ultimate freedom.
You are an extremist now. An aesthetic voyager whose home is the road.
[PAUSE 2s]

[AMBIENT: light wind through metal shell | vol 0.14]
[VOICE]
You find it—an abandoned Fairbanks bus. Rusted. Beautiful. Your sanctuary.
"Hello? Is there anybody here?" Your voice echoes. No answer.
You howl at the sky. You carve into wood: Alexander Supertramp. May 1992.
[PAUSE 1.5s]

[AMBIENT: soft crowd murmur | vol 0.12]
[VOICE]
Flashback. Graduation day. Your parents smile, proud and oblivious. You see their young faces—about to marry, innocent.
You want to warn them... but you don't. Instead you say: "Do what you are going to do, and I will tell about it."
Your father offers a new car. "That Datsun could blow up any day."
"I don't want a new car. I don't want anything. These things, things, things."
You measure yourself by a fierce code. You know you'll break away—with characteristic immoderation.
[PAUSE 2s]

--- ACT TWO — THE JOURNEY (min 6-12) ---

[AMBIENT: campfire + distant ocean | vol 0.16]
[VOICE]
On the road, you meet Rainey and Jan. Rubber tramps with open hearts.
"Where are your mom and dad?" Jan asks.
"Living their lives somewhere."
"You look like a loved kid. Be fair."
You quote Thoreau: "Rather than love, than money, than faith, than fame, than fairness—give me truth."
Rainey by the fire: "It is important not necessarily to be strong, but to feel strong. To face the blind, deaf stone alone with nothing but your hands and your head."
[PAUSE 2s]

[AMBIENT: river rapids rising | vol 0.2]
[VOICE]
You need a permit to paddle the river. Twelve years' wait.
"Twelve years? To paddle down a river?" You don't wait.
You launch anyway. "I'm Supertramp!" You ride the rapids—no permission, no helmet, no fear.
You paddle to Mexico. A sandstorm steals your kayak. You walk back north, sand in your teeth.
[PAUSE 1.5s]

[AMBIENT: grain elevator hum | vol 0.14]
[VOICE]
In South Dakota, you work the combines. Wayne sees through the bravado.
"You can't juggle blood and fire forever," he says. "No Alaska till spring. Head south, kid."
[PAUSE 1.5s]

[AMBIENT: low tension drone | vol 0.1]
[VOICE]
Flashback. The family secret.
Your father was already married when he met your mother. You and Carine—bastard children. The revelation murders every day's truth.
Violence. Rage. You make Carine promise silence. The road becomes necessary.
[PAUSE 2s]

[AMBIENT: desert wind + light tinnitus | vol 0.12]
[VOICE]
Ron Franz finds you in the desert. An eighty-year-old leatherworker, lonely, grieving.
"Don't you think you ought to be getting an education?"
"Careers are a twentieth-century invention. I'm living like this by choice."
You take him climbing. He's terrified, but does it—for you.
At the summit, he gasps, alive.
"When you forgive, you love. And when you love, God's light shines on you."
"What do you say you let me adopt you?" he asks.
You promise: after Alaska, you'll talk.
[PAUSE 2s]

--- ACT THREE — ALASKA & RECKONING (min 13-20) ---

[AMBIENT: harsh wind + distant crow | vol 0.18]
[VOICE]
You shoot your first big game. Triumph surges. You can survive.
But you wait too long. Flies. Maggots. The meat spoils.
You scream at the sky: "Where are the animals now? I'm hungry!"
[PAUSE 1.5s]

[AMBIENT: brittle winter hush | vol 0.14]
[VOICE]
You forage. Hedysarum alpinum—wild potato root. Safe, you think.
You eat. Days later, you're violently ill. You misidentified. Or the season was wrong. The river's too high. You're too weak to walk out.
[PAUSE 2s]

[AMBIENT: near-silence + heartbeat | vol 0.08]
[VOICE]
In your journal, your handwriting shakes.
"Happiness only real when shared."
You think of Ron. Rainey. Jan. Wayne. Tracy. Your parents—imperfect, human.
You were right about society's sickness. Wrong about the cure. The cure was not isolation. It was connection. Forgiveness.
[PAUSE 2.5s]

[AMBIENT: interior bus creak + soft wind | vol 0.1]
[VOICE]
Your body fails. You crawl into your sleeping bag.
"I have had a happy life and thank the Lord. Goodbye and may God bless all."
Images flood your mind: your mother's face; your father's hand; Carine's laughter; the ocean with Rainey; Ron at the summit; Jan knitting you a hat; Wayne teaching you to combine.
You see what could have been: returning home, older, wiser, ready to forgive—and be forgiven. But it is too late now.
Your last thought: "What if I were smiling and running into your arms? Would you see then what I see now?"
[PAUSE 3s]

--- CLOSING ---

[AMBIENT: faint, warm drone | vol 0.1]
[VOICE - soft, concluding]
Carine, voice over:
"You wrote your story, Chris. Two years you walked the earth. You found what you were looking for, and what you didn't know you needed until it was too late.
You died alone in that bus. But your story makes us remember. It makes us forgive. It makes us love.
Because that's what you learned, isn't it?
That happiness is only real when shared."

[FADE OUT — silence]
